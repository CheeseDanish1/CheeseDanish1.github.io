Words and Definitions for Period 4

  • Louisiana Purchase

    A land acquisition in 1803 in which the United States purchased approximately 827,000 square miles of land from France for $15 million, doubling the size of the country.

  • War of 1812

    A conflict between the United States and Great Britain from 1812-1815, primarily over issues related to trade and maritime rights.

  • Embargo Act of 1807

    A law passed by Congress that prohibited U.S. ships from trading with foreign ports, in an attempt to remain neutral during the Napoleonic Wars in Europe.

  • Missouri Compromise

    A law passed in 1820 that admitted Missouri as a slave state and Maine as a free state, and prohibited slavery in territories north of 36Â°30' latitude.

  • Trail of Tears

    The forced removal of thousands of Native Americans from their ancestral lands in the Southeast to Indian Territory in present-day Oklahoma in the late 1830s, resulting in the deaths of thousands due to disease, starvation, and exposure.

  • Nullification Crisis

    A political crisis in 1832-1833 in which South Carolina attempted to nullify a federal tariff law, asserting the state's right to nullify any federal law it deemed unconstitutional.

  • Second Great Awakening

    A religious revival in the early 19th century that emphasized personal salvation and social reform, leading to the growth of new religious denominations and social movements such as temperance and abolition.

  • Monroe Doctrine

    A policy announced by President James Monroe in 1823 that declared the Western Hemisphere off-limits to European colonization or interference, asserting the United States' dominance in the region.

  • Seneca Falls Convention

    The first women's rights convention held in Seneca Falls, New York, in 1848, where participants issued the Declaration of Sentiments, calling for women's suffrage and other reforms.
