Memories of Mary Anne

A 19th Century American Persona

Entries from my journal

July 4th, 1836

I am Mary Anne, a 20-year-old woman living in Boston, Massachusetts. I work as a seamstress in a local shop. Today is Independence Day, and the city is alive with festivities. People are out in the streets, waving flags and cheering. This year, there is a lot of talk about the upcoming presidential election. The Whig Party has nominated William Henry Harrison, and the Democrats have nominated Martin Van Buren. I am not very interested in politics, but I have heard that the economy is not doing well, and many people are struggling to find work.

January 2nd, 1845

It has been almost ten years since I wrote in this journal. I am now 29 years old, married to my husband, John, and living in Cincinnati, Ohio. We moved here a few years ago to start a new life. The city is growing rapidly, thanks in part to the Ohio River, which allows goods to be transported easily. One of the most exciting things to happen recently is the completion of the Miami and Erie Canal. This new transportation system will make it easier to move goods across the state. However, I am concerned about the issue of slavery, which continues to divide the nation.

March 10th, 1850

It has been a few years since my last entry, and a lot has happened. John and I now have three children, and we are still living in Cincinnati. I have been following the debates in Congress about the Compromise of 1850. The Compromise is an attempt to resolve the issue of slavery in the new territories that the United States acquired from Mexico. The Fugitive Slave Act, which is part of the Compromise, requires citizens to assist in the capture of runaway slaves. This law is deeply troubling to me, and I worry about the impact it will have on free black people in the North.

October 12th, 1862

The country is at war. John is fighting for the Union Army, and I am doing my best to support the war effort from home. I am sewing uniforms and bandages for the soldiers. I am also trying to keep up with the news from the front lines. The Battle of Antietam was fought last month, and it was one of the bloodiest battles in American history. The Union army managed to repel the Confederate forces, but the cost was high. I pray for John's safety every day.

November 5th, 1876

It has been a few years since the war ended. John made it home safely, but he is not the same man he was before he left. He suffers from nightmares and has trouble sleeping. I have been doing my best to support him, but it is not easy. The country is still dealing with the aftermath of the war. Reconstruction has been difficult, and there are still tensions between the North and the South. However, there is also a sense of hope for the future. The country is changing rapidly, with new technological advances and social movements. I am curious to see what the future will bring.
