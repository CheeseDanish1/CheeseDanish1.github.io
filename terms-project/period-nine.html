Words and Definitions for Period 3

  • Reaganomics

    A a set of economic policies implemented during the 1980s by President Ronald Reagan that involved tax cuts, deregulation, and reduced government spending with the goal of stimulating economic growth.

  • War on Drugs

    A campaign initiated by President Ronald Reagan in the 1980s to combat drug abuse and trafficking. It involved increased law enforcement efforts, harsher penalties for drug offenses, and a public awareness campaign.

  • Globalization

    The increasing interconnectedness of economies, cultures, and societies around the world, driven by advancements in technology and communication.

  • NAFTA

    The North American Free Trade Agreement, signed in 1994 by the United States, Canada, and Mexico, which eliminated tariffs and other trade barriers between the three countries.

  • September 11th attacks

    A series of terrorist attacks carried out by al-Qaeda on September 11, 2001, in which nearly 3,000 people were killed. The attacks led to a heightened focus on national security and the War on Terror.

  • Affordable Care Act

    A healthcare reform law passed in 2010 that aimed to increase access to healthcare while reducing costs. It is also known as Obamacare.

  • LGBTQ+ Rights

    The movement to secure legal and social equality for LGBTQ+ individuals. This has included efforts to decriminalize homosexuality, secure marriage equality, and protect against discrimination based on sexual orientation and gender identity.

  • Black Lives Matter

    A social movement that emerged in response to the killing of unarmed black individuals by police. It aims to address systemic racism and promote social justice for black people.

  • Climate Change

    The long-term alteration of global weather patterns caused by human activities such as burning fossil fuels, deforestation, and agriculture. It has led to rising sea levels, increased frequency and intensity of natural disasters, and other environmental and economic impacts.

  • COVID-19 pandemic

    A global pandemic caused by the novel coronavirus that emerged in 2019. It has resulted in millions of deaths, widespread economic disruption, and changes in daily life, such as mask-wearing and social distancing.
