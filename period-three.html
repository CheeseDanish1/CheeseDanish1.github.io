Words and Definitions for Revolutionary Era

  • Proclamation of 1763

    A British proclamation that banned colonial settlement west of the Appalachian Mountains, following the French and Indian War.

  • Stamp Act

    A British tax on printed materials, imposed on the colonies in 1765, leading to widespread protest and resistance.

  • Boston Massacre

    A deadly confrontation between British soldiers and Boston colonists in 1770, in which five colonists were killed.

  • Tea Act

    A British law in 1773 that gave the British East India Company a monopoly on tea sales in the colonies, leading to the Boston Tea Party.

  • Intolerable Acts

    A series of punitive laws passed by the British Parliament in 1774 in response to the Boston Tea Party, which closed Boston Harbor and suspended Massachusetts' self-government.

  • Declaration of Independence

    A document written by Thomas Jefferson and adopted by the Continental Congress on July 4, 1776, declaring the United States' independence from Great Britain.

  • Articles of Confederation

    The first constitution of the United States, adopted by the Continental Congress in 1777, which created a weak central government with limited powers.

  • Whiskey Rebellion

    A 1794 uprising of Pennsylvania farmers against a federal excise tax on whiskey, which was ultimately put down by federal troops.

  • Northwest Ordinance

    A law passed by Congress in 1787 that established a system for creating new states in the Northwest Territory and prohibited slavery in the region.

  • Treaty of Paris

    The 1783 treaty that officially ended the Revolutionary War, recognized American independence, and established the boundaries of the new United States.
