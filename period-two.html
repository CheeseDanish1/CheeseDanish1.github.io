Words and Definitions for Period 2

  • Powhatan Confederacy

    The Native American alliance of tribes in Virginia that interacted with and sometimes clashed with the Jamestown settlers.

  • House of Burgesses

    The first elected legislative assembly in the American colonies, established in Virginia in 1619.

  • Indentured Servitude

    A labor system in which individuals worked for a period of time in exchange for passage to the New World.

  • Bacon's Rebellion

    A 1676 uprising in Virginia led by Nathaniel Bacon against Governor William Berkeley's administration, primarily over Native American policy.

  • Slavery

    The practice of forced labor, particularly in the Southern colonies, where African slaves were brought to work on plantations.

  • Triangle Trade

    A three-way trade route that involved the transportation of goods, including slaves, between Europe, Africa, and the Americas.

  • Great Awakening

    A religious revival in the 1730s and 1740s that spread through the American colonies and led to the establishment of new denominations.

  • Navigation Acts

    British laws that regulated colonial trade and restricted the colonies from trading with other countries.

  • French and Indian War

    A conflict from 1754-1763 between France and Great Britain over control of the North American continent, with Native American allies on both sides. Albany Plan of Union: A proposal in 1754 by Benjamin Franklin for the colonies to unite under a central government to better manage relations with Native Americans and defend against the French.
