Words and Definitions for Period 6

  • Jim Crow Laws

    A series of state and local laws in the late 19th and early 20th centuries that enforced racial segregation and discrimination against African Americans in the Southern United States.

  • Plessy v. Ferguson

    A Supreme Court case in 1896 that upheld the constitutionality of racial segregation under the "separate but equal" doctrine, setting the stage for decades of legal discrimination.

  • Homestead Act

    A federal law passed in 1862 that granted 160 acres of public land to any person who would farm it for at least five years, encouraging westward expansion and settlement.

  • Transcontinental Railroad

    A railroad system that connected the eastern and western coasts of the United States, completed in 1869, which facilitated trade, transportation, and migration.

  • Indian Wars

    A series of conflicts between the U.S. government and various Native American tribes in the West from the late 1860s to the early 1890s, resulting in the forced relocation of many tribes to reservations.

  • Dawes Act

    A federal law passed in 1887 that aimed to assimilate Native Americans into American culture by dividing tribal lands into individual allotments and granting citizenship to those who adopted "civilized" lifestyles.

  • Chinese Exclusion Act

    A federal law passed in 1882 that prohibited Chinese immigration to the United States, marking the first significant law restricting immigration based on nationality.

  • Sherman Antitrust Act

    A federal law passed in 1890 that aimed to limit the power of monopolies and promote competition in the marketplace, but was often used to suppress labor unions and small businesses.

  • Spanish-American War

    A conflict fought between the United States and Spain in 1898 over Spanish colonial territories, resulting in U.S. victory and the acquisition of Puerto Rico, Guam, and the Philippines.

  • American Federation of Labor

    A national labor organization founded in 1886 that focused on achieving higher wages, shorter hours, and better working conditions through collective bargaining.
