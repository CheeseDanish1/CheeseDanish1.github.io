Words and Definitions for Age of Discovery

  • Native Americans

    Indigenous peoples of North America prior to European contact

  • European Exploration

    The period of time when European explorers sailed across the Atlantic Ocean to explore and colonize the New World.

  • Columbian Exchange

    The exchange of plants, animals, and diseases between the Old World and the New World following the arrival of Europeans in the Americas.

  • Spanish Conquest

    The conquest and colonization of the Americas by the Spanish, beginning with Columbus' arrival in 1492 and continuing with the conquest of the Aztec and Inca empires.

  • English Colonization

    The establishment of English colonies in North America, including the Virginia Colony, Massachusetts Bay Colony, and others.

  • Jamestown

    The first permanent English settlement in North America, established in 1607 in what is now Virginia.

  • Pilgrims

    A group of English settlers who established the Plymouth Colony in present-day Massachusetts.

  • Fur Trade

    The exchange of furs for European goods between Native Americans and Europeans.

  • Encomienda System

    A system used by the Spanish in their colonies in the Americas, which granted Spanish settlers the right to demand tribute and labor from Native Americans in exchange for protection.

  • Mercantilism

    An economic theory that held that a country's wealth was based on its holdings of gold and silver, and that the goal of economic policy should be to increase exports and limit imports in order to accumulate wealth.
