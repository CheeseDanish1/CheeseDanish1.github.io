Words and Definitions for Progressive Era

  • Populism

    A political movement that emerged in the late 19th century advocating for the rights and interests of farmers, workers, and other marginalized groups, and challenging the power of big business and political elites.

  • Spanish Flu

    A pandemic that spread across the world in 1918-1919, killing an estimated 50 million people, including approximately 675,000 Americans.

  • World War I

    A global conflict fought from 1914 to 1918 involving many of the world's major powers, including the United States, which entered the war in 1917 on the side of the Allies.

  • Treaty of Versailles

    A peace treaty signed in 1919 that ended World War I and imposed harsh penalties on Germany, which many historians believe contributed to the outbreak of World War II.

  • Roaring Twenties

    A period of economic prosperity, cultural change, and social liberation in the United States from the end of World War I to the onset of the Great Depression.

  • New Deal

    A series of economic and social reforms implemented by President Franklin D. Roosevelt in response to the Great Depression, including government intervention in the economy and the establishment of social welfare programs.

  • Dust Bowl

    A period of severe dust storms and ecological disaster in the Great Plains region of the United States during the 1930s, caused by a combination of drought and poor land management practices.

  • World War II

    A global conflict fought from 1939 to 1945, involving many of the world's major powers, including the United States, which entered the war after the Japanese attack on Pearl Harbor in 1941.

  • Holocaust

    A genocide carried out by Nazi Germany during World War II, resulting in the systematic murder of approximately six million Jews, as well as other groups considered "undesirable" by the Nazis.

  • Atomic Bomb

    A weapon of mass destruction that uses nuclear reactions to release enormous amounts of energy, which was first used by the United States in 1945 to bomb the Japanese cities of Hiroshima and Nagasaki, leading to Japan's surrender and the end of World War II.
